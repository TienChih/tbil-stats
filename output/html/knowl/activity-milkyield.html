Activity 6.4.6. Chicken Feed.

Dairy farmers wish to test a new type of diets for dairy cows, with the hope that the new diet will increase milk yields. 20 cows are fed the traiditional diet and 15 the experimental diet. The cows are observed over a 4 week period, and the average daily milk yield and sample standard deviations for both groups are recorded in Liters.

\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{|c|c|c|c|} \hline \textbf{Diet} \amp n \amp \bar{x} \amp s\\ \hline \text{Standard} \amp 20\amp 42.3 \amp 8.73\\ \text{Experimental} \amp 15\amp 45.1 \amp 6.24\\ \hline \end{array} \end{equation*}

Let \(\mu_1\) denote the true mean of milk production for cows on the standard diet, and \(\mu_2\) denote the true mean of milk production for cows on the experimental diet.

(a)

Which of the following best describes the null hypothesis \(H_0\text{?}\)

  • \(\mu_1-\mu_2\lt 0\) liters.
  • \(\mu_1-\mu_2> 0\) liters.
  • \(\mu_1-\mu_2\neq 0\) liters.
  • \(\mu_1-\mu_2= 0\) liters.
(b)

Which of the following best describes the alternative hypothesis \(H_A\text{?}\)

  • \(\mu_1-\mu_2\lt 0\) liters.
  • \(\mu_1-\mu_2> 0\) liters.
  • \(\mu_1-\mu_2\neq 0\) liters.
  • \(\mu_1-\mu_2= 0\) liters.
(c)

Find the standard error \(SE\text{.}\)

(d)

Find \(t\)-score for \(\bar{x_1}-\bar{x_2}\text{.}\)

(e)

Compute a \(p\)-value (be sure to use the appropriate level of significance).
