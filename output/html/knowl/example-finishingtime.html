Example 6.2.10. Running Times.

In Exploration 6.2.3, we had an alternative hypothesis that the average running time was less than 95 minutes. This gives us:

  • \(H_A:\mu\lt 95\text{.}\)

  • \(H_0:\mu= 95\text{.}\)

We were able to compute \(\bar{x}=82.6\text{,}\) \(s\approx 23.50006\text{.}\) Given that \(n=20\) we are able top find \(SE\approx 5.25477\text{.}\)

In (c), we assumed \(\mu=95\text{,}\) the null hypothesis, then found that the \(t_{\bar{x}}\) value was

\begin{equation*} t_{\bar{x}}=\frac{82.6-95}{5.25477}\approx −2.35976\text{.} \end{equation*}

In (d), we computed the \(p\)-value by finding \(P(t\lt −2.35976)\approx 0.01457\) in a \(t\)-distribution with 19 degrees of freedom.
