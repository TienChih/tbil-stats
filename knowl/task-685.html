Task 5.3.7.b.

IF adult male heights were normally distributed, we would expect it to be approximated by a normal random variable \(X\) with mean \(\mu=m\) and standard deviation \(\sigma=std\text{.}\) Confirm that if we subdivide a normal distribution into intervals of length \(std\text{,}\) we would have:

\begin{align*} P(X\lt m-3std)\amp \approx 0.0014\\ P(m-3std\leq X\lt m-2std)\amp \approx 0.0214\\ P(m-2std\leq X\lt m-std)\amp \approx 0.1359\\ P(m-std\leq X\lt m)\amp \approx 0.3413\\ P(m\leq X\lt m+std)\amp \approx 0.3413\\ P(m+std\leq X\lt m+2std)\amp \approx 0.1359\\ P(m+2std\leq X\lt m+3std)\amp \approx 0.0214\\ P(X\geq m+3std)\amp \approx 0.0014 \end{align*}
