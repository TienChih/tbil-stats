Then we compute

\begin{equation*} \chi^2=Z_1^2+\cdots Z_k^2=\frac{(n_1-E_1)^2}{E_1}+\cdots+\frac{(n_k-E_k)^2}{E_k}. \end{equation*}

This removes the distinction between over and undershooting the expectations, and sums the adjusted error for all the values, which gives a measure for the “total” deviation from the theoritical expectation. (Think Activity 1.5.3, Definition 1.5.1.)
